Workforce Development and Education

Educating the public health workforce on how to leverage genomic technologies to investigate and respond to infectious diseases threats is a key pillar of the Pathogen Genomics Centers of Excellence (PGCoE) Network. Educational opportunities include broad, national initatives spearheaded by the Lead PGCoE for Education, as well as trainings and tutorials conducted and made available by all individual PGCoE sites. These resources and educational videos span core pathogen genomics principles, effective laboratory protocols, and state-of-the-art analytical tools.

Lead PGCoE for Education

Build your skills in pathogen genomics with Pathogen Genomics for Public Health, an online education program with free, self-paced courses designed for:

  • Current public health workforce, including laboratorians, bioinformaticians, and epidemiologists
  • Future workforce, including fellows, trainees, and students
  • Clinicians and infection preventionists
  • Researchers and other interested learners
  • Program directors and laboratory leaders

Join a community of learners building the skills to turn pathogen genomic insights into public health action. As pathogens and outbreaks continue to evolve, these skills are increasingly essential for effective infectious disease response and biosurveillance.

  • Start with Foundations I: Conceptual Foundations of Pathogen Genomics, an introduction for anyone in public health, or with a general interest in understanding pathogen genomics. In this course, you will discover what pathogen genomics is and why it is becoming an essential tool for tracking infectious diseases and responding to outbreaks.

  • Continue with Foundations II: Technical Introduction to Pathogen Genomic Epidemiology: Mutations, Transmission, and Phylogenetics for a deeper understanding of key molecular biology principles that underpin genomic epidemiology. You will explore how sequencing pathogen genomes can reveal insights into pathogen transmission—laying the groundwork for more advanced topics.

Together, these courses explain how sequencing data supports surveillance and outbreak response and offer concrete tools to improve collaboration across lab, bioinformatics, and epi teams. Learners hear directly from leaders in the field— including Alli Black (Washington), Arianna Miles-Jay (Michigan), Pardis Sabeti (The Broad Institute), and Shirlee Wohl (Massachusetts), plus practitioners from California, Kansas, Michigan, Utah, and the FDA—reflecting a truly national pathogen genomics community.

These courses were developed under the Lead Education activity of the Pathogen Genomics Centers of Excellence (PGCoE) with funding from CDC OAMD. Input and expertise from partners across the PGCoE network have been central at every stage of development—from priority‑setting and content design to pilot testing and refinement. Foundations I and II are the gateway to a planned curriculum of 10+ courses, including advanced modules for those seeking deeper expertise. The next three courses are under development, as a pathway, “Applied Pathogen Genomics for Public Health: Sequencing Strategy, Study Design, and Interpretation”—will cover sequencing strategy (including metagenomics), genomics-informed study design, and genomic data interpretation.
